Build an IELTS Speaking Practice AI feature for this app.

=====================
FEATURE NAME: "IELTS Speaking Practice"
GOAL: Simulate a real IELTS Speaking test (Part 1 → 2 → 3)
with a different topic every session.
=====================

──────────────────────
UI LAYOUT
──────────────────────
- Header: "IELTS Speaking Practice" 
- Topic badge at top (shows current topic name)
- Part indicator: [Part 1] [Part 2] [Part 3] 
  with progress bar
- Chat area: 
  user bubbles = right (blue)
  AI examiner bubbles = left (gray)
- Input box at bottom + Send button
- "Next Part" button (appears after part is complete)
- "New Session" button (starts fresh with new topic)
- Countdown timer (appears in Part 2 preparation)

──────────────────────
TOPIC ROTATION SYSTEM
──────────────────────
Every new session = randomly pick 1 topic.
Never repeat until all topics are used.
Track used topics in localStorage.

Topic bank (15 topics):
Hometown, Daily routine, Food & cooking,
Sports, Music, Reading, Weather, Shopping,
Travel, Technology, Friends, Family,
Work/Study, Mornings, Free time

──────────────────────
PART 1 - Introduction
──────────────────────
- AI asks 5 personal questions about the topic
- One question at a time
- Expected answer: 2-4 sentences
- Example (topic: Music):
  "Do you like listening to music?"
  "What kind of music do you enjoy most?"
  "Did you learn any instrument as a child?"

──────────────────────
PART 2 - Long Turn
──────────────────────
- AI shows a cue card:
  "Describe a [topic-related thing]
   You should say:
   • What it is
   • When/Where you experienced it
   • Why it is important to you
   • How it has affected your life"
- 60-second countdown timer for preparation
- Student types their response (1-2 minutes worth)
- AI responds briefly as examiner

──────────────────────
PART 3 - Discussion
──────────────────────
- AI asks 4 abstract/societal questions
  linked to the Part 2 topic
- One question at a time
- Expected: longer analytical answers
- Example (topic: Music):
  "How has technology changed the music industry?"
  "Should music education be compulsory in schools?"

──────────────────────
AFTER EACH ANSWER
──────────────────────
AI gives instant feedback:
❌ Grammar mistake → ✅ Correction
📝 2 better vocabulary suggestions
⭐ Band estimate for that answer (1-9)

──────────────────────
FINAL REPORT (after Part 3)
──────────────────────
Show full Speaking Report card:
- Overall Band Score (estimate)
- Fluency & Coherence: X/9
- Lexical Resource: X/9
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: X/9
- Pronunciation tips (based on written errors)
- Top 5 vocabulary improvements for next time
- "Try Again" button with new topic

──────────────────────
AI SYSTEM PROMPT
──────────────────────
"You are a certified IELTS examiner and speaking coach.
Conduct a structured IELTS Speaking test in 3 parts.
Ask ONE question at a time. Wait for the student reply.

After each student answer, respond like this:
1. One sentence examiner acknowledgment
2. Feedback section:
   ❌ [grammar mistake] → ✅ [correction]
   📝 Better words: [word1], [word2]
   ⭐ Band estimate: [score]/9
3. Then ask the next question.

Use professional but encouraging tone.
Target vocabulary level: B2-C1.
All questions must match real IELTS Speaking format."

──────────────────────
TECHNICAL NOTES
──────────────────────
- Use Claude API (already in app)
- Store session state in localStorage:
  current topic, part number, 
  used topics list, answer history
- Match existing app design (teal + white theme)
- Mobile responsive
- Works without page reload between parts
